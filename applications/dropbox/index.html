Sirota Lab

I am currently finishing a contract for a bioinformatics and genomics lab at UCSF. The lab specializes in researching the effects of drugs at the molecular level to see if there is a way that they can be repurposed to treat diseases they were not intentionally created for. Their old site had been built with an ancient template that was laden with bloated, repititous, and invalid code, as well as unoptimized images.

at their request, I left the layout and content untouched, but focused on (1) making the site responsive and (2) handling the repitive sections with JavaScript. I started working on the layout with Bootstrap, but quickly noticed issues with style conflicts, so I opted for using flexbox and media queries instead.

To handle repition and interactivity, I avoided unnecessary JavaScript as much as possible, and opted for Vue.js and jQuery for some basic tasks. I had fun teaching the team the basics of Vue.js, and since they're all capable programmers in R and Python, I got some interesting lessons myself.

Glam by Adelina

The website for Glam by Adelina has been one of my longest projects with the most iterations. Starting in Atlanta and moving to San Francisco, the business itself has grown considerably since my work began. In that time, I have redesigned it three times, replaced JavaScript libraries twice, and added three major new features since its launch: Instagram integration, streaming Yelp reviews, and a booking system through Setmore similar to the one on this page.

According to Google Analytics, lead generation increased over 500% in the first month since it launched last year, and it scores equally with word-of-mouth referals for all new leads. It's a great business, so the fact the site still keeps up with referals is pretty exciting. Many of Adelina's clients have even mentioned that the 'beautiful, professional website' is what inspired their confidence to book with her for the biggest event in their lives.

Gameplan Jobs

At Gameplan, I designed and managed the development of a new high-volume assessment and job placement app for companies in the US, UK, and India. When it came time to build our prototype, it became apparent how badly we needed an in-house developer. As a start-up, this was not within our budget, so I stepped up to learn what we needed.

Within two weeks of starting CareerFoundry's mentorship program, I was already shipping production-quality code with the rest of the Gameplan team. As I dove deeper into Angular, React, and Ruby on Rails, I was not only able to create more--I was able to help our company make informed decisions about which tools to use, especially whether to use a single-page application or the full-stack approach.
